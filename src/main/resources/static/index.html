System Protocols

STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES

1. Geometric Consistency

Every scan must use the exact same volume (e.g., 10ml syringe) and identical containers. Slight variations in depth drastically alter light absorption and skew AI inference.

2. Thermal Stability

Liquid density changes with temperature, affecting optical scattering. Ensure training datasets and live presentation samples are stabilized at identical room temperatures before testing.

3. "Blank" Calibration

The TCS3200 sensor reads ambient air and plastic. Always establish an absolute-zero baseline using 10ml of distilled water to mathematically remove environmental interference.

4. Homogenization Effect

Suspended particles in liquids sink or float over time. Always vigorously stir the main sample immediately before drawing it into the testing syringe to ensure a true representation.

5. Zero Contamination

A microscopic film of previous liquid will destroy dataset purity. The testing container must be thoroughly rinsed with water and wiped bone-dry between every single scan.
